Why Japan?

Japanese culture and cuisine are rich and intertwined. Dishes like sushi, ramen, and tempura are staples of Japanese cuisine, made with fresh ingredients and known for their simplicity and flavor. One of the key features of Japanese cuisine is the use of seasonal ingredients, which makes the food both fresh and healthy.

At the same time, traditions like kimono, sumo, and the tea ceremony are integral to Japanese culture. Items such as Kokeshi dolls and fans are beautiful examples of Japanese craftsmanship. In Japan, simplicity and minimalism are present in both cooking and art, with a strong focus on aesthetic value and harmony.
